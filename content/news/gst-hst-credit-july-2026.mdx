The **GST/HST credit July 2026** payment landed with a meaningful upgrade this summer: as of this month, the tax-free quarterly benefit that millions of low- and modest-income Canadians rely on has been folded into the new Canada Groceries and Essentials Benefit (CGEB), and the amounts are roughly 25% higher than a year ago. If you filed your 2025 taxes, the new, richer figure was calculated for you automatically — no application, no forms for most people. Here is exactly what changed, how much you can receive, and when the money arrives.

The GST/HST credit has always been a tax-free payment issued four times a year — in July, October, January, and April — meant to offset some of the sales tax that lower-income households shoulder. Every July it recalculates for a fresh benefit year based on your previous tax return. This July is different: the payment is the same idea, but bigger, rebranded, and locked in at the higher level for several years. For anyone stretching a tight budget, that is a rare piece of genuinely helpful news.

## The Key Numbers

Here is the fast version, all for the July 2026–June 2027 benefit year, which is calculated from your 2025 tax return.

| Detail | Figure |
|---|---|
| New maximum, single adult | $679/year |
| New maximum, married/common-law couple | $890/year |
| New maximum, per child under 19 | $234/year |
| Increase vs. former GST/HST credit | ~25% (locked in through 2031) |
| First payment of the benefit year | July 3, 2026 |
| Next quarterly payment | October 5, 2026 |
| Income phase-out begins (adjusted family net income) | $45,521 |

The one-line read: it is the same dependable quarterly benefit you already know, now paid under the Canada Groceries and Essentials Benefit name and worth about a quarter more.

## What Changed for the GST/HST Credit July 2026

The headline is that the **GST/HST credit July 2026** payment is no longer, strictly speaking, "the GST/HST credit." In January 2026 the federal government announced the [Canada Groceries and Essentials Benefit](https://globalnews.ca/news/11948946/canada-groceries-and-essentials-benefit-payment-july-3/) — a program that builds directly on the old GST/HST credit but pays about 25% more. The January and April 2026 payments were the last ones issued under the old name; the July payment is the first regular quarterly instalment of the enriched benefit.

A few things did not change, and that matters. It is still tax-free. It is still paid four times a year. It is still income-tested and calculated straight from your tax return. And you still do not have to apply if you file your taxes. What changed is the size of the cheque and the label on it. The government has said the 25% top-up will stay in place through 2031, so this is not a one-off bonus — it is the new baseline for the next several years.

There was also a one-time advance in early June 2026, worth roughly half of a household's annual benefit, to push money out the door ahead of the regular schedule. If you saw an unexpected deposit in June, that is very likely what it was.

## How Much You'll Get — and the Income Limits

The maximums above are exactly that: maximums. The benefit is income-tested, so what actually reaches your account depends on your adjusted family net income (AFNI) from your 2025 return. Households below the phase-out threshold get the full amount; above it, the benefit shrinks steadily.

Here is how the new maximums compare with the old GST/HST credit figures.

| Household | Former GST/HST credit (max) | New CGEB (max, 2026–27) |
|---|---|---|
| Single adult | ~$533 | $679 |
| Married/common-law couple | ~$698 | $890 |
| Each child under 19 | ~$184 | $234 |

Phase-out works like this: once your adjusted family net income passes $45,521, the credit is reduced by 5 cents for every dollar above that line. In practice, the benefit tapers to zero somewhere in the upper $50,000s for a single person and stretches into the $60,000s or low $70,000s for larger families with children — the more people in your household, the higher the income at which you still qualify for something. Because the amount is income-tested, there is no single figure that applies to everyone. The only reliable way to see yours is to check your CRA My Account or the benefit notice the CRA mails out.

## The Payment Schedule

The benefit keeps the familiar quarterly rhythm. Here are the dates that matter for the current benefit year.

| Payment | Date |
|---|---|
| One-time advance top-up | Early June 2026 |
| First quarterly payment | July 3, 2026 |
| Next quarterly payment | October 5, 2026 |
| Following payment | January 2027 |
| Final payment of the benefit year | April 2027 |

If the total you qualify for is small — under a set annual threshold — the CRA may pay it as a single lump sum in July rather than splitting it into four. Payments arrive by direct deposit if you are set up for it, or by cheque in the mail otherwise. Direct deposit is faster and worth switching on through CRA My Account so you are not waiting on the post.

## Who Qualifies and How to Get It

Eligibility is refreshingly simple. You generally qualify if you are a resident of Canada for tax purposes, are 19 or older (with some exceptions for younger people who have a spouse, common-law partner, or child), and your family income falls under the phase-out ceiling. Around [12 million Canadians](https://www.bnnbloomberg.ca/business/economics/2026/07/03/rollout-of-canadas-boosted-grocery-benefit-starts-today/) receive the benefit.

The single most important step is also the easiest: file your tax return. The CRA uses your 2025 return to calculate the benefit automatically, so even if you had no income to report, filing is what triggers the payment. For most people there is no separate application at all. Newcomers to Canada are the main exception — they may need to complete a short form (the RC151) to get set up in their first year.

If you believe you should have received a payment and did not, check that your marital status, mailing address, and direct-deposit details are current in CRA My Account. An out-of-date file is the usual culprit behind a missing or delayed deposit.

## The Bottom Line

The **GST/HST credit July 2026** payment is one of the few clearly good pieces of news in an otherwise tight year for Canadian households: the same dependable quarterly benefit, now paid as the Canada Groceries and Essentials Benefit and worth about 25% more, with that increase locked in through 2031. New maximums of $679 for a single adult, $890 for a couple, and $234 per child mean a little more breathing room for roughly 12 million people. The action items are simple — file your taxes, keep your CRA details current, and mark July 3 and October 5 on the calendar.
