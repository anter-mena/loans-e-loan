**Money Mart vs Cash Money** compares two established Canadian high-cost lenders, but the best choice is sometimes neither. Their advertised payday-loan fee can be similar while application access, product availability, funding, payment methods and support differ. Start with the total repayment and your next-pay budget—not the closest branch or fastest claim.

## Money Mart vs Cash Money at a Glance

| Feature | Money Mart | Cash Money |
|---|---|---|
| Access | Online, app and branch services | Online, phone and branch services |
| Payday-loan fee shown in current examples | $14 per $100 where the provincial disclosure applies | $14 per $100 where the provincial disclosure applies |
| Funding | Depends on product, approval and selected channel | e-Transfer in as little as 15 minutes or direct deposit for eligible online loans |
| Payment | Set by the agreement and product | AutoPay or eligible manual methods |
| Other services | Instalment lending and multiple in-store financial services | Lines of credit, other lending and branch services where available |
| Geographic limits | Product-specific and provincial | Product-specific and provincial |

This table deliberately avoids declaring a universal winner. A Money Mart branch may offer a service that its online flow does not; a Cash Money product may be unavailable in a particular province. The signed agreement controls.

A responsible **Money Mart vs Cash Money** comparison therefore begins with the same province, product type, amount and repayment period on both sides.

## 1. Confirm You Are Comparing the Same Product

Both sites offer more than one financial product. A payday loan, instalment loan and line of credit can have completely different annual rates, payment schedules and eligibility rules. A comparison becomes meaningless if one browser tab shows a two-week payday product and the other shows a multi-month loan.

For both quotes, write down:

- legal lender and product name;
- principal and cash received;
- cost per $100 or annual percentage rate;
- every mandatory and optional fee;
- payment dates and number of payments;
- total amount repayable;
- late or failed-payment consequences;
- early-payment rule.

Do not let the monthly payment hide a longer term. Money Mart's page includes multiple products and optional protection information; Cash Money also presents products beyond payday loans. Remove optional coverage from the comparison unless it is wanted, then evaluate its cost, exclusions and cancellation terms separately.

## 2. Calculate What $14 per $100 Means

When the applicable payday-loan price is $14 per $100, the fee scales directly:

| Amount borrowed | Fee | Total due |
|---:|---:|---:|
| $200 | $28 | $228 |
| $300 | $42 | $342 |
| $500 | $70 | $570 |
| $750 | $105 | $855 |

The same headline price means the base dollar cost is tied when the amount and term structure are truly identical. It does not mean both agreements are identical. Provincial schedules, optional products, funding methods and failed-payment treatment can still differ.

FCAC describes payday loans as an expensive form of short-term credit. Its $300, two-week example converts the $42 fee to approximately 365% annualized. Use APR to compare credit types and use $342 to test the immediate payday.

If the full total cannot be repaid without another loan, lower the amount or stop. Our [fast-borrowing guide](/blog/where-can-i-borrow-money-fast-in-canada) starts with options that may avoid high-cost credit entirely.

## 3. Compare Branch, App, Phone and Online Support

Money Mart emphasizes its branch network, mobile app and online presence. Cash Money offers online and phone applications and operates physical locations. A reader may prefer a branch for cash-related services or document help, while another needs a fully remote application.

Test support before borrowing. Ask the same question through the channel you expect to use: “What is the exact total repayment and when is it due?” A clear answer should match the written disclosure. Confirm how to retrieve statements, submit a complaint and request accessibility support.

Use only the official domain or a branch located through the official locator. Search advertisements and map results can contain impersonators or stale phone numbers. A real storefront name does not validate a person contacting you through WhatsApp or text.

## 4. Verify Province and Eligibility Before Sharing Data

Payday-loan rules and availability vary across Canada. The lender must be authorized where required in the borrower's province. Money Mart and Cash Money publish province-specific disclosures and licence details, but the relevant record is the one for your location and product.

Use the federal [payday-lending directory](https://ised-isde.canada.ca/site/office-consumer-affairs/en/money-debt-and-giving/payday-lending-find-information-you-need) to reach provincial information. Match the legal name and licence number rather than only a trading name.

Then check eligibility without assuming approval. Common requirements can include age of majority, Canadian residence, identity, regular income, contact information and an active bank account. Never alter a statement or income figure. A legitimate decline is safer than a contract based on false information.

## 5. Audit Funding and Payment Methods

Cash Money currently advertises e-Transfer funding in as little as 15 minutes for eligible online loans and also lists direct deposit. Money Mart's funding route depends on the selected product and channel. Ask when an approval becomes final and when the first payment is taken.

Speed language is conditional. A transfer can be delayed by identity review, incomplete information, bank processing or technical limits. Do not commit to a penalty with a biller based on the best-case marketing time.

For **Money Mart vs Cash Money**, funding speed should break a tie only after cost, authorization and affordability have passed review.

For repayment, confirm whether the lender uses a pre-authorized debit, e-Transfer, bank card, branch payment or another method. Ask whether it retries a failed debit and whether your bank could also charge an NSF fee. Save every authorization.

Never send a preliminary e-Transfer to “activate” the loan. A lender or impersonator requesting insurance, tax, processing or release money before giving you proceeds is a major scam warning. Do not confuse receiving loan funds by e-Transfer with sending money to qualify.

## 6. Run the Reduced-Paycheque Test

This **Money Mart vs Cash Money** test protects the next pay period from a comparison based only on branches, apps or advertised transfer time.

A household expects $1,900 on payday and considers a $500 loan with $570 due. Its normal essential spending for that pay period is $1,500. After repayment, only $1,330 remains—$170 short of essentials.

The loan therefore postpones the original problem and creates another one. A second payday loan would add another fee without repairing the deficit.

Use this worksheet:

1. Write confirmed net income for the repayment period.
2. Subtract the complete lender payment.
3. Subtract housing, food, utilities, medication and transportation.
4. Subtract existing minimum debt payments.
5. Add a modest unexpected-expense amount.

A negative answer means the proposed amount is unaffordable even if the lender approves it. If an existing balance is already difficult, contact the lender before the due date. Our guide on [payday-lender legal action](/blog/can-a-payday-loan-company-sue-you-canada) explains why ignoring a valid debt is not a solution.

## 7. Compare Alternatives With the Same Worksheet

Ask the creditor for a due-date change or split payment before borrowing. Compare an employer advance, credit-union small loan, bank overdraft, line of credit, credit-card cash advance and help from family under a written plan. Include every fee and interest charge rather than assuming a familiar product is cheap.

For example, if a utility grants a seven-day extension for no charge, neither payday lender wins. If an overdraft costs $5 plus interest and is already approved, it may cost less than $42 on a $300 payday advance. If neither is available and the expense is essential, borrow the smallest amount that closes the verified gap—not the maximum offered.

Government benefits, community programs and non-profit credit counselling can be relevant when the shortage repeats. Payday credit can change timing; it cannot solve an income-expense imbalance.

## How This Comparison Was Evaluated

We used each lender's Canadian payday-loan and fee disclosures as of July 20, 2026, then checked the general cost and licensing context against FCAC. We did not submit applications, accept funds or rely on affiliate commissions. That means the comparison can verify published structures but cannot predict an approval, personalized limit or exact funding time.

Recheck all material facts at application. Save the page showing the province, capture the disclosure offered before signing and compare it with this article's worksheet. If a lender changes a fee, channel or availability rule, the current official disclosure takes priority.

Reader reviews can identify recurring service questions, but they cannot prove what a future borrower will experience. Use reviews to create questions for support—not to replace the agreement, regulator record or personal budget. This separation between published facts, individual offers and user opinion keeps the comparison reproducible.

Use Money Mart's branch, app and product range only if those features matter to the actual need. Use Cash Money's remote and repayment options only if the written terms fit better. Do not award either lender points for a feature you will not use.

The responsible **Money Mart vs Cash Money** conclusion comes from the final disclosure: same product, same amount, same province and same repayment period. If the totals are tied, choose on support, access and payment control. If the reduced-paycheque test fails, choose neither and pursue a lower-cost timing solution.
